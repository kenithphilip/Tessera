# Tessera v1.1.0-alpha roadmap

**Status**: Draft. v1.0.x stays the supported line until the
3-maintainer / 2-organisation gate
([`tessera_sandbox_stage.md`](./tessera_sandbox_stage.md)) clears
and the OpenSSF Sandbox application lands.

## What v1.1 is for

v1.0 froze the wire format and the load-bearing primitives. v1.1
is the first release line under foundation governance. The shape
of the release is therefore process-led, not feature-led: the
goal is to ship under a Steering Committee whose decisions are
captured in ADRs, not in maintainer head-state.

The five v1.1 milestones below are NOT committed dates. They are
the activation order. Each milestone has an explicit gate; v1.1.0
ships when M5 closes.

## Milestone gates

| Milestone | Gate | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **M1** OpenSSF Sandbox accepted | `ossf/tac` PR for `tessera_sandbox_stage.md` merges. | Maintainer + 2 co-maintainers |

## Steering Committee CFP (draft)

Open this CFP after M1 closes, not before. Posting it before the
sandbox application lands creates an ambiguous chicken-and-egg
("apply for OpenSSF needing 3 maintainers" vs "recruit
maintainers needing OpenSSF acceptance").

### Call for participation: Tessera Steering Committee (3-5 members)

> Tessera is the reference implementation of two security
> primitives for LLM agent systems: signed trust labels with
> taint-tracking, and schema-enforced dual-LLM execution. v1.0
> shipped 2026-04-25 and the project has been accepted into the
> OpenSSF Sandbox. We're forming the initial Steering Committee
> that will govern v1.1 and onward.
>
> **What the SC does**
>
> - Approves new ADRs and major API changes (majority vote).
> - Owns the release schedule and the security disclosure
>   process.
> - Reviews and approves new co-maintainers.
> - Represents Tessera at the OpenSSF TAC and sponsoring WG.
>
> **What we're looking for in candidates**
>
> - Substantive contribution to AI agent security, MCP, OpenSSF
>   workstreams, or downstream consumers (LangChain / LlamaIndex /
>   PydanticAI / CrewAI middleware).
> - Willingness to attend monthly SC meetings (~1h) and quarterly
>   WG reports.
> - One-year initial term, renewable. No pay; this is volunteer.
>
> **Composition target**
>
> - 3-5 members
> - At least 2 distinct organisational affiliations (not
>   counting "Independent")
> - At least one maintainer of a downstream framework or a
>   foundation-model provider org
>
> **How to apply**
>
> Open an issue at
> <https://github.com/kenithphilip/Tessera/issues> tagged
> `governance:sc-application` with a 3-5 sentence statement of
> interest, your relevant background, and your affiliation.
> Applications close 30 days after this CFP is posted.
>
> Questions: kenith.philip@fivetran.com or the public tracking
> issue at <https://github.com/kenithphilip/Tessera/issues/19>.

## What v1.1 does NOT change

- Wire format (frozen at v1.0; v2 changes are gated on
  [`docs/adr/0007-provenance-label-v2-migration.md`](../adr/0007-provenance-label-v2-migration.md)).
- License (Apache-2.0 for the library; AGPL for the AgentMesh
  service per [ADR-0001](../adr/0001-license-split.md)).
- Threat model (the indirect-prompt-injection scope in
  [SECURITY.md](../../SECURITY.md) holds).
- The 2-primitive substrate. New code in v1.1 composes on top;
  it does not modify `Context.min_trust`, `WorkerReport`, or
  signed labels at rest.

## Forward feature list (no committed dates)

Items below are candidates for v1.1.x point releases under SC
governance. They are written here so the Steering Committee
inherits a settled candidate set, not a blank queue.

- **TPS-001 Critic v2 budget governor.** Per-session circuit
  breaker tuned by the SC; ships when v1.0 telemetry from real
  deployments arrives.
- **TPS-002 Multi-org delegation chaining.** Extend
  `DelegationToken` chain length past 2 with auditable hop
  attestation. Gated on a downstream consumer demonstrating
  need.
- **TPS-003 ProvenanceLabel v2 design study.** ADR work, not
  shipping code. Captures field-shape changes discovered during
  Phase 3 principles v2.
- **TPS-004 Eval-as-product harness for community probes.**
  Bundle the existing `corpus/probes/` red-team corpus with a
  reproducible runner so external auditors can re-run scorecards
  end-to-end.
- **TPS-005 Adapter conformance suite.** Pin a per-framework
  conformance contract for the LangChain / LlamaIndex /
  PydanticAI / CrewAI adapters so future framework upgrades
  don't drift the Tessera surface silently.
- **TPS-006 Drift-monitor hook for production deployments.**
  Surface `MCP_DRIFT_*` events as a Prometheus exporter without
  forcing OTel adoption.

The SC owns prioritisation. Items can be cut, deferred, or
re-scoped via ADR.
